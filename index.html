Why Web Dev?

>>>Growing up in Myanmar, I always had a passion for engineering and architecture, inspired by my uncle who was the only educated person I knew back then. However, due to circumstances, I found myself attending medical school without enjoying it. During this time, I had doubts about my path(tried to quit 2 times during academic years and once even after becoming a doctor), but it led me to meet my wife, who I consider the best part of that journey. Therefore, I never regret joing med school if you ask me. Following her to the UK, I attempted to join the NHS but unfortunately, it didn't work out. This setback, coupled with the onset of the pandemic, took a toll on my mental health(2020 was the toughest year of my life).

Figthing back the hardship, I decided to pursue a career that genuinely excited me and resonated with my initial passion for problem-solving and creating things: programming. I took up jobs in retail(2021) and grocery(2022) in the interim, while dedicating my free time to learning programming. Self-studying from resources like Udemy, CS50, and freeCodeCamp, I have been honing my skills for the past year. I find joy and a sense of belonging in coding that I didn't find in medicine. Now, I'm eager to turn this passion into a professional career.<<<<
